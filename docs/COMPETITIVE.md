# StoneBC — Competitive Positioning

## Market Landscape

The cycling app market is dominated by global platforms optimized for connected, urban environments. None serve rural cycling well. StoneBC occupies a defensible position as the **hyperlocal expert layer** — the app that knows a specific region's roads, conditions, and hazards the way a global platform never will.

---

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Strava ($7.99/mo subscription)
**Their strength:** Social network for athletes. Segments, leaderboards, kudos, training logs. 120M+ users globally.

**Their weakness:** Generic everywhere. Same experience in NYC and Needles Highway. Requires connectivity for core features. No local knowledge — doesn't know which trails are closed, where cell dies, or which routes connect. No group communication. No safety features for remote riding.

**StoneBC advantage:**
- Curated routes by locals vs. crowdsourced noise
- Offline-first (all routes bundled, works without signal)
- Rally Radio (P2P voice, no cell needed)
- Cell dead zone mapping
- USFS closure integration
- Emergency SOS
- Tour guides with local stops and context

**Positioning:** Strava tracks your ride. StoneBC knows the road.

---

### Komoot ($29.99 one-time for region bundle)
**Their strength:** Route planning with turn-by-turn navigation. Sport-specific routing (road, MTB, gravel). Strong in Europe.

**Their weakness:** Algorithm-generated routes — no local vetting. Requires connectivity for planning. No group communication. No safety features. Weaker US coverage.

**StoneBC advantage:**
- Hand-curated routes vs. algorithm-generated
- Rally Radio for group coordination
- Multi-day tour guides with stops, checklists, local context
- Expedition Journal for ride documentation
- Cell coverage + emergency features

**Positioning:** Komoot calculates routes. StoneBC's routes were ridden first.

---

### Ride with GPS ($7.99/mo)
**Their strength:** Large route library. Good planning tools. Popular with touring cyclists.

**Their weakness:** Library is user-uploaded (quality varies wildly). No local curation or vetting. No group comms. No safety layer. Standard subscription model.

**StoneBC advantage:**
- Quality over quantity — every route vetted and current
- Co-op community model vs. corporate platform
- Offline-native (not just offline caching)
- Integrated safety features
- Tour guide system beyond just route lines

**Positioning:** Ride with GPS has routes. StoneBC has guides who know them.

---

### AllTrails ($35.99/yr)
**Their strength:** Massive trail database. Strong hiking community. Good discovery UI. Offline maps.

**Their weakness:** Hiking-first, cycling second. Trail data skewed toward foot traffic. No cycling-specific features (elevation profiles for bikes, road surface type). No group comms.

**StoneBC advantage:**
- Purpose-built for cycling (road, gravel, MTB, bikepacking)
- Rally Radio for group rides
- Bike-specific data (surface type, traffic, shoulders)
- Community bike co-op integration
- Tour guides designed for multi-day bike trips

**Positioning:** AllTrails is for hikers. StoneBC is for riders.

---

### TrailForks (Free / $3.99/mo)
**Their strength:** MTB trail database. Trail conditions reporting. Popular with mountain bikers.

**Their weakness:** MTB-only — no road, gravel, touring, or bikepacking. Limited outside MTB trail systems. No group features.

**StoneBC advantage:**
- All cycling disciplines (road, gravel, MTB, touring, bikepacking, commuting)
- Multi-day touring capabilities
- Community bike program
- Broader safety features for remote riding

**Positioning:** TrailForks is for the trail. StoneBC is for the whole ride.

---

## The Defensible Moat

StoneBC's competitive advantage is **not** technology — any of these companies could build offline maps or P2P radio. The moat is:

1. **Local knowledge** — Curated routes, mapped dead zones, USFS integration. This takes boots on the ground, not just data.
2. **Community ownership** — Co-op model means the community IS the product. Users are stakeholders, not data points.
3. **Regional depth** — Owning one state deeply beats covering every state shallowly.
4. **Trust** — When you're 40 miles from pavement with no signal, you trust the app built by someone who's been there.

Global platforms can't replicate this because it doesn't scale the way VC-backed companies need it to. That's the point — StoneBC scales through **partnership replication**, not global homogeneity.

## Pricing Strategy

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|---------|
| Free | $0 | Route browsing, basic ride recording, community feed, The Quarry |
| Premium | $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr | Turn-by-turn navigation, Rally Radio, offline maps, tour guides, expedition journal, advanced ride stats |

**Why undercut Strava:** StoneBC isn't replacing Strava — many users will have both. Price below Strava ($7.99) to make it an easy add-on, not a hard choice. The value prop is "this plus Strava" not "this instead of Strava."
