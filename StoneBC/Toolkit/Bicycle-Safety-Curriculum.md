# Bicycle Safety Curriculum

## Overview

This curriculum covers bicycle safety education for riders of all ages and skill levels. It includes classroom instruction, on-bike skills practice, and road riding components.

---

## Program Options

### By Format

| Format | Duration | Best For |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Quick Safety Talk** | 15-30 min | Events, EAB graduation, drop-ins |
| **Basic Safety Class** | 1-2 hours | General public, new riders |
| **Comprehensive Course** | 4-8 hours | Serious commuters, youth programs |
| **Kids Learn-to-Ride** | 2-4 hours | Children learning to ride |
| **Family Bike Safety** | 2-3 hours | Parents + children together |

### By Audience

| Audience | Focus Areas | Special Considerations |
|----------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Children (5-10)** | Balance, basic rules, helmet fit | Fun, games, short attention |

| **Adults - Experienced** | Advanced traffic, commuting | May have bad habits |
| **Seniors** | Balance, visibility, route selection | Physical limitations, e-bikes |

## Quick Safety Talk (15-30 minutes)

Use at events, EAB graduations, or as a refresher.

### ABC Quick Check (5 minutes)

**A - Air**
- Check tire pressure (squeeze test or gauge)
- Look for cuts, bulges, wear
- Proper inflation: firm but gives slightly

**B - Brakes**
- Squeeze each lever - does bike stop?
- Check pad wear and alignment
- Cables not frayed?

**C - Chain & Cranks**
- Chain lubricated, not rusted?
- Spin cranks - smooth?
- Pedals secure?

**Quick Release / Bolts**
- Wheels secure?
- Seat tight?
- Handlebars aligned and tight?

### Helmet Fit (3 minutes)

**The 2-V-1 Rule:**
- **2 fingers** above eyebrows (level, not tilted back)
- **V** shape straps around ears
- **1 finger** between chin and strap

*Demonstrate on volunteer, have participants check each other*

### Be Seen, Be Predictable (5 minutes)

**Visibility:**
- Lights: white front, red rear (required at night)
- Reflectors on wheels, pedals
- Bright/reflective clothing helps

**Predictability:**
- Ride in straight line (no weaving)
- Signal turns
- Make eye contact with drivers
- Don't surprise anyone

### Basic Hand Signals (2 minutes)

- **Left turn:** Left arm straight out
- **Right turn:** Left arm up at 90° OR right arm straight out
- **Stopping:** Left arm down at 90°, palm back

*Have everyone practice signals*

### Top 5 Rules (5 minutes)

1. **Wear a helmet** - Every ride, properly fitted
2. **Ride with traffic** - Same direction as cars
3. **Obey traffic signs/signals** - Stop signs, red lights
4. **Be visible** - Lights, bright clothes, eye contact
5. **Stay alert** - No headphones, watch for hazards

### Q&A (remaining time)

## Basic Safety Class (1-2 hours)

### Materials Needed

- Bikes for demonstration
- Helmets (extras for those without)
- Cones or chalk for practice area
- Handouts (rules, hand signals)
- First aid kit

### Agenda

| Time | Topic | Method |
|------|-------|--------|
| 0:00 | Welcome, introductions | Discussion |
| 0:10 | Why bike safety matters | Statistics, stories |
| 0:20 | Helmet fit | Demo + practice |
| 0:30 | ABC Quick Check | Demo + hands-on |
| 0:45 | Rules of the road | Presentation |
| 1:00 | Hand signals | Demo + practice |
| 1:10 | Hazard awareness | Discussion + images |
| 1:25 | On-bike skills (if time/space) | Practice |
| 1:45 | Q&A, resources, wrap-up | Discussion |

### Module: Why Safety Matters (10 min)

**Statistics (adapt to local data):**
- ~1,000 bicyclists killed annually in US
- ~130,000 injured in traffic crashes
- Head injuries are leading cause of bike fatalities
- Helmets reduce head injury risk by ~50%
- Most crashes are preventable

**Discussion questions:**
- Has anyone been in a bike crash? What happened?
- What concerns you about riding?
- What's the most dangerous situation you've encountered?

**Key message:** Most crashes are preventable with proper equipment, knowledge, and behavior.

### Module: Helmet Fit (10 min)

**Why helmets work:**
- Absorbs impact energy
- Distributes force across larger area
- Only works if worn correctly

**Fitting steps:**
1. Position level, 2 fingers above eyebrows
2. Adjust side straps to form V under ears
3. Tighten chin strap - 1 finger fits
4. Shake head - helmet shouldn't move
5. Push up on front - shouldn't tilt back

**Common mistakes:**
- Tilted back (forehead exposed)
- Too loose (moves around)
- Straps too loose
- Wrong size helmet

**When to replace:**
- After any crash (even if no visible damage)
- Every 5 years (materials degrade)
- If cracked, dented, or damaged

*Activity: Pair up and check each other's fit*

### Module: ABC Quick Check (15 min)

Expand on quick talk version with hands-on practice.

**A - Air**
- Proper pressure varies by tire (check sidewall)
- Road bikes: 80-120 PSI
- Mountain bikes: 30-50 PSI
- Hybrid/commuter: 50-70 PSI
- Under-inflation: harder to pedal, pinch flats, poor handling
- Over-inflation: harsh ride, blowout risk

*Activity: Check tire pressure on demo bike, discuss what good vs. bad feels like*

**B - Brakes**
- Should stop bike within reasonable distance
- Pads should contact rim squarely (or rotor for disc)
- No rubbing when not applied
- Cables should move freely

*Activity: Test brakes on demo bike, show pad alignment*

**C - Chain, Crank, Cassette**
- Chain should be lubricated (not dry, not gunky)
- No stiff links
- Cranks spin smoothly
- Shifting works (if geared)

*Activity: Examine chain condition, demonstrate lubrication*

**Quick releases and bolts**
- Front and rear wheels secure
- Seat post secure
- Handlebars tight and aligned

*Activity: Practice opening/closing quick release*

### Module: Rules of the Road (15 min)

**Bicycles are vehicles**
- Same rights AND responsibilities as cars
- Must follow traffic laws
- Can use full lane when necessary

**Ride WITH traffic**
- Same direction as cars
- Never ride against traffic (3x more dangerous)
- Motorists don't expect wrong-way riders

**Lane positioning**
- Ride as far right as PRACTICABLE (not possible)
- Exceptions: turning left, avoiding hazards, lane too narrow to share
- "Taking the lane" is legal and sometimes safest

**Intersections**
- Most crashes happen at intersections
- Position yourself to be seen
- Make eye contact with drivers
- Don't pass on right at intersections

**Right of way**
- Stop signs: full stop, yield to traffic
- Traffic signals: obey like a car
- Crosswalks: may walk bike, or ride through where legal
- Yield to pedestrians always

**Sidewalk riding**
- Check local laws (varies by jurisdiction)
- If legal: yield to pedestrians, slow speed, announce passing
- Generally not recommended for adults (driveways = danger)

*Discussion: What are the bike laws in our city/state?*

### Module: Hand Signals (10 min)

**Why signal:**
- Required by law
- Communicates intent
- Makes you predictable
- Eye contact + signal = safest

**Standard signals:**
| Action | Signal |
|--------|--------|
| Left turn | Left arm extended straight |
| Right turn | Left arm bent up at 90° OR right arm straight |
| Slowing/stopping | Left arm bent down at 90° |
| Hazard | Point down at hazard |

**When to signal:**
- Before turning (100+ feet in advance)
- Before changing lanes
- When slowing unexpectedly
- To warn other cyclists of hazards

**Practical tips:**
- Signal early, but return hand to bars before maneuvering
- Don't signal if you need both hands for control
- Eye contact + signal = best communication

*Activity: Everyone practices signals, quiz each other*

### Module: Hazard Awareness (15 min)

**Road hazards:**
| Hazard | Danger | Response |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Potholes | Wheel/tire damage, crash | Scan ahead, avoid or bunny hop |
| Grates/Gaps | Wheel caught | Cross perpendicular or avoid |
| Railroad tracks | Wheel caught | Cross at 90° angle |
| Gravel/Sand | Loss of traction | Slow down, stay upright |
| Wet leaves | Very slippery | Avoid or go very slow |
| Painted lines | Slippery when wet | Avoid when wet |
| Glass/Debris | Flat tire | Scan, avoid |

**Traffic hazards:**
| Hazard | Danger | Response |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Dooring | Car door opens into path | Ride outside door zone (3+ feet from parked cars) |
| Right hook | Car turns right across your path | Watch for turn signals, slow at intersections |
| Left cross | Car turns left into you | Make eye contact, be visible |
| Passing too close | Sideswiped | Take lane if necessary, stay alert |
| Backing vehicles | Struck from behind | Watch driveways, parking lots |

**The door zone:**
- #1 danger for urban cyclists
- Ride at least 3 feet from parked cars
- Scan for occupants (heads, brake lights)
- It's legal to ride outside door zone even if it means taking lane

*Activity: Look at photos/video of hazards, discuss responses*

### Module: Being Seen (5 min)

**Lighting requirements:**
- Most states: white front light, red rear light/reflector at night
- "Night" = sunset to sunrise, or low visibility
- Be visible from 500+ feet

**Beyond requirements:**
- Daytime running lights (flashing) very effective
- Side visibility: spoke reflectors, wheel lights
- Reflective ankle bands (motion catches attention)
- Bright/fluorescent clothing

**Positioning for visibility:**
- Don't hide behind parked cars
- Take the lane when necessary
- Position at intersections to be seen
- Make eye contact

## Comprehensive Course (4-8 hours)

Includes everything in Basic class plus:

### Additional Modules

**Route Planning (30 min)**
- Using bike maps and apps
- Identifying bike infrastructure
- Choosing routes for safety vs. speed
- Alternative routes for different conditions

**Traffic Negotiation (45 min)**
- Advanced intersection positioning
- Lane changes in traffic
- Roundabouts
- Multi-lane roads
- Highway-style interchanges

**Emergency Maneuvers (45 min - on bike)**
- Quick stop (controlled emergency braking)
- Instant turn (avoiding sudden obstacle)
- Rock dodge (small obstacle avoidance)
- Scanning (looking back while riding straight)

**Group Riding (30 min)**
- Single file vs. two abreast
- Communication (verbal + hand signals)
- Paceline basics
- Riding with children

**Night Riding (30 min)**
- Lighting setup
- Route selection
- Clothing and visibility
- Hazards unique to night

**Weather Conditions (30 min)**
- Wet weather: braking, visibility, traction
- Wind: positioning, drafting safely
- Heat: hydration, sun protection
- Cold: layering, extremity protection

**Commuting Skills (30 min)**
- Carrying cargo
- Clothing for work
- Bike parking and security
- Multi-modal (bike + transit)

### On-Bike Skills Practice

**Controlled environment exercises:**

| Skill | Setup | Progression |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Straight line | Chalk line or tape | Ride within 6" of line |
| Slow riding | Cones 20' apart | Ride as slow as possible without foot down |
| Stopping | Line on ground | Stop with front wheel on line |
| Scanning | Look back at instructor's hand signals | Call out number of fingers while riding straight |
| Quick stop | Signal to stop | Stop as quickly as possible without skidding |
| Avoidance | Obstacle appears suddenly | Swerve around safely |

**Road riding (supervised):**
- Group ride on quiet streets
- Practice intersections, turns, lane positioning
- Instructor provides feedback

## Kids Learn-to-Ride (2-4 hours)

For children who cannot yet ride a bicycle.

### Age Guidelines

| Age | Approach |
|-----|----------|
| 3-4 | Balance bikes only |
| 5-6 | Balance method, transition to pedals |
| 7+ | Balance method or traditional |

### The Balance Method

Research shows balance-first approach is faster and safer than training wheels.

**Equipment:**
- Bike sized correctly (child can touch ground with feet flat)
- Remove pedals temporarily
- Lower seat so feet flat on ground
- Helmet properly fitted

**Progression:**

**Stage 1: Walking (15-20 min)**
- Straddle bike, walk forward
- Focus: steering, balance while moving
- Praise effort, keep it fun

**Stage 2: Gliding (20-30 min)**
- Push off, lift feet, coast
- Start on slight downhill if available
- Goal: longer glides, feet up longer
- Practice stopping (feet down)

**Stage 3: Feet Up (20-30 min)**
- Extended gliding, feet on frame or out
- Steering while gliding
- Controlled stops using brakes

**Stage 4: Add Pedals (when gliding confidently)**
- Reinstall pedals
- Start with one foot on pedal, push off
- Begin pedaling while gliding
- Celebrate success!

**Tips for instructors:**
- Never hold the child or bike (creates dependency)
- May run alongside for encouragement
- Keep sessions short (30 min max for young children)
- End on success, even if small
- Patience - every child learns at own pace

### After Riding: Basic Safety

Once child can ride, cover:
- Helmet every time
- Riding in safe areas (sidewalk, path, driveway)
- Stopping and looking before crossing
- Watching for cars backing up
- Riding in control (not too fast)

## Family Bike Safety Class (2-3 hours)

Parents and children learn together.

### Agenda

| Time | Topic | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 0:00 | Welcome, parent/child introductions | Build community |
| 0:15 | Why bike together? Benefits discussion | Engagement |
| 0:30 | Helmet fit - kids fit parents, parents fit kids | Fun activity |
| 0:45 | ABC Quick Check - families work on own bikes | Hands-on |
| 1:15 | Rules for kids: where/how to ride | Age-appropriate |
| 1:30 | Rules for parents: riding with children | Teaching tips |
| 1:45 | BREAK | Snacks if available |
| 2:00 | Practice ride - controlled area | Skills stations |
| 2:30 | Route planning - safe routes in neighborhood | Discussion |
| 2:45 | Resources, Q&A, wrap-up | Handouts |

### Key Content: Riding with Children

**Where to ride with kids:**
- Sidewalks (where legal) until age 10
- Multi-use paths
- Quiet residential streets
- Bike lanes on slow streets

**Positioning:**
| Child Age/Skill | Parent Position |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| New rider | Behind (to supervise) |
| Gaining confidence | Beside (for guidance) |
| Confident child | In front (to model) OR behind (to supervise) |
| Multiple children | Adults front and back |

**Teaching moments:**
- Point out hazards as you approach
- Narrate what you're doing ("I'm signaling left")
- Practice stops and signals regularly
- Let them lead sometimes (builds confidence)
- Praise specific behaviors

**Setting rules:**
- Always stop at corners/driveways
- Stay on sidewalk/path (or street rules)
- Stay within sight/voice range
- Wear helmet every time (parents too!)
- No riding after dark

## Youth Program Safety Integration

Safety content for ongoing youth programs (EAB, bike clubs, etc.)

### Session 1: Start with Safety (30 min)

- Helmet fit (they keep helmet if EAB)
- ABC Quick Check (do on their project bike)
- Shop safety rules
- Basic road rules introduction

### Session 2: Rules Deep Dive (20 min)

- Traffic laws for cyclists
- Hand signals practice
- Local laws specific to your area

### Mid-Program: Hazard Awareness (20 min)

- Road hazards identification
- Door zone demo
- "What would you do?" scenarios

### Pre-Graduation: Road Skills (30 min)

- Practice ride in controlled area
- Route planning for their commute
- Night visibility (if applicable)

### Graduation: Safety Pledge

Participants sign pledge committing to:
- Wear helmet every ride
- Obey traffic laws
- Use lights at night
- Keep bike maintained
- Ride predictably

## Instructor Guidelines

### Qualifications

| Level | Requirements |
|-------|--------------|
| **Assistant** | Complete safety class + shadow experienced instructor |
| **Instructor** | League Cycling Instructor (LCI) cert OR equivalent training + experience |
| **Lead Instructor** | LCI cert + 10+ classes taught + curriculum development ability |

### League Cycling Instructor (LCI) Certification

The League of American Bicyclists offers LCI certification:
- 2-day seminar
- Written and riding exam
- Best practice for serious safety education
- [bikeleague.org/ridesmart/become-instructor](https://bikeleague.org/content/become-instructor)

### Teaching Tips

**General:**
- Know your audience (age, experience level)
- Start with what they know
- Be positive, not preachy
- Use stories and examples
- Hands-on beats lecture
- Check for understanding frequently

**For children:**
- Keep it fun (games, activities)
- Short segments (10-15 min max)
- Lots of movement
- Positive reinforcement
- Simple language
- Repetition is okay

**For adults:**
- Respect their experience
- Ask about their concerns
- Practical application focus
- Avoid condescension
- Acknowledge that rules may feel inconvenient

**For anxious riders:**
- Extra patience
- Start very basic
- Controlled environment first
- Build confidence gradually
- Celebrate small wins
