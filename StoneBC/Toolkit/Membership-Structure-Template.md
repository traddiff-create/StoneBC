# Membership Structure Template

## Overview

This document outlines common membership models for bicycle cooperatives. Choose and adapt the model that best fits your community's needs and your organization's capacity.

---

## Membership Philosophy

Bicycle cooperatives typically balance two values:
1. **Accessibility** - Making services available to all, regardless of income
2. **Sustainability** - Generating revenue to maintain operations

Most co-ops use a hybrid approach: tiered memberships, sliding scales, and earn-your-membership options.

---

## Common Membership Models

### Model A: Simple Flat Rate

**Best for:** Small co-ops, simple operations

| Membership Type | Annual Fee | Benefits |
|-----------------|-----------|----------|
| Individual | $[50] | Shop access, voting, discounts |
| Family/Household | $[75] | Same benefits, multiple people |
| Student/Senior | $[30] | Same benefits, reduced rate |

**Pros:** Simple to administer, easy to understand
**Cons:** May exclude low-income community members

---

### Model B: Tiered Access

**Best for:** Co-ops with varying service levels

| Tier | Annual Fee | Shop Hours | Parts Discount | Classes |
|------|-----------|------------|----------------|---------|
| Basic | $[25] | 2/month | 0% | Pay per class |
| Standard | $[50] | Unlimited | 10% | 1 free/quarter |
| Premium | $[100] | Unlimited + priority | 15% | Unlimited |
| Supporter | $[200]+ | All Premium benefits + recognition |

**Pros:** Revenue from those who can pay more
**Cons:** More complex administration

---

### Model C: Sliding Scale

**Best for:** Equity-focused co-ops

| Income Level | Suggested Fee | Notes |
|--------------|---------------|-------|
| Full price | $[60] | Suggested for those with means |
| Reduced | $[30] | No questions asked |
| Minimal | $[10] | Barrier-free option |
| Free | $0 | By request or EAB completion |

**Note:** "Pay what you can" messaging, honor system

**Pros:** Maximum accessibility, community-centered
**Cons:** Less predictable revenue

---

### Model D: Time-Based (Punch Card)

**Best for:** Drop-in focused operations

| Option | Price | Value |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Single visit | $[10] | 3 hours shop time |
| 5-visit punch | $[40] | $8/visit |
| 10-visit punch | $[70] | $7/visit |
| Monthly unlimited | $[50] | Unlimited visits |
| Annual unlimited | $[150] | Best value |

**Pros:** Flexible for occasional users
**Cons:** Requires tracking system

---

### Model E: Hybrid Cooperative

**Best for:** True member-owned cooperatives

| Member Type | Buy-In | Annual Dues | Voting | Patronage |
|-------------|--------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| Owner-Member | $[100] | $[25] | Yes | Yes |
| User-Member | $0 | $[50] | No | No |
| Supporting | $0 | $[100]+ | No | No |

**Owner-members:**
- One-time equity buy-in (can be paid over time)
- Voting rights on major decisions
- Eligible for patronage refunds
- May serve on board

**Pros:** True cooperative ownership, engaged members
**Cons:** More complex legal/financial structure

## Youth & Family Programs

### Youth Membership (Under 18)

| Option | Fee | Requirements |
|--------|-----|--------------|
| Youth Individual | $[15] | Parent/guardian consent |
| Youth EAB Graduate | Free | Complete Earn-A-Bike |
| Family Add-On | $[10] ea | With adult membership |

### Family Membership

- Covers household members at one address
- Typically 1.5x individual rate
- All members share benefits
- One vote per household or per adult (define clearly)

## Membership Administration

### Sign-Up Process

1. **Application** - Contact info, emergency contact, membership type
2. **Payment** - Collect fee or set up work trade
3. **Waiver** - Liability release signed
4. **Orientation** - Shop rules, safety, tour
5. **Card/Certificate** - Proof of membership issued

### Tracking Systems

| System | Cost | Best For |
|--------|------|----------|
| Spreadsheet | Free | Small co-ops (<100 members) |
| Wild Apricot | $$$ | Growing co-ops, automation |
| MemberPress | $$ | WordPress-based |
| Square for Retail | $$ | Integrated with POS |
| Paper cards | Free | Simplest option |

### Renewal Process

- Send reminder 30 days before expiration
- Grace period: [30] days
- Lapsed members: must re-apply or pay back dues

## Legal Considerations

### For Standard Memberships
- Membership agreement/terms of service
- Liability waiver
- Privacy policy (if collecting data)

### For Cooperative Memberships
- Membership/ownership agreement
- Disclosure of rights and responsibilities
- Patronage dividend policy
- Transfer/redemption of shares

**Consult an attorney for cooperative membership structures.**

## Evaluation Questions

Review your membership structure annually:

1. Are we meeting revenue goals?
2. Is membership growing or declining?
3. Are low-income community members able to participate?
4. Is administration manageable?
5. Do members feel they get value?
6. What feedback have we received?
7. Should we adjust pricing or benefits?
