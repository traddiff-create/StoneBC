# Grant Writing Guide for Bicycle Cooperatives

## Overview

Grants can provide significant funding for bicycle cooperatives, especially for specific programs, equipment, and capacity building. This guide covers how to find, apply for, and manage grants.

---

## Types of Grants

### By Funder Type

| Funder Type | Typical Range | Best For | Competition |
|-------------|---------------|----------|-------------|
| **Community Foundations** | $1,000-25,000 | General operating, programs | Moderate |
| **Corporate Foundations** | $1,000-50,000 | Programs aligned with business | Moderate |
| **Family Foundations** | $5,000-100,000 | Mission-aligned projects | Varies |
| **Government (Local)** | $5,000-50,000 | Community programs, youth | Moderate |
| **Government (State)** | $10,000-250,000 | Transportation, health | High |
| **Government (Federal)** | $50,000-500,000+ | Major initiatives | Very High |

### By Purpose

| Purpose | Examples |
|---------|----------|

## Finding Grants

### Local Sources (Start Here)

1. **Community Foundation**
   - Search "[Your City/County] Community Foundation"
   - Most have online grant databases
   - Often fund local nonprofits broadly

2. **United Way**
   - Local chapter may have grants
   - Often focused on health, education, financial stability

3. **Corporate Giving Programs**
   - Local banks (Wells Fargo, local credit unions)
   - Outdoor retailers (REI, local bike shops)
   - Healthcare systems (community health funds)
   - Utilities (often have community funds)

4. **City/County Government**
   - Community development block grants (CDBG)
   - Youth services funding
   - Transportation/sustainability funds
   - Tourism/economic development

## Grant Readiness Checklist

Before applying, ensure you have:

### Legal/Organizational
- [ ] 501(c)(3) status OR fiscal sponsor with 501(c)(3)
- [ ] EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- [ ] Articles of incorporation
- [ ] Bylaws
- [ ] Board of directors list with affiliations
- [ ] Conflict of interest policy

### Financial
- [ ] Annual budget (current year)
- [ ] Financial statements (last 2-3 years if available)
- [ ] 990 tax return (if filed)
- [ ] List of current funders
- [ ] Audit or financial review (if required)

### Programmatic
- [ ] Mission statement
- [ ] Program descriptions
- [ ] Outcomes data (if available)
- [ ] Logic model or theory of change
- [ ] Organizational history/background

### Administrative
- [ ] Key staff bios
- [ ] Organizational chart
- [ ] Annual report (if available)
- [ ] Letters of support (from partners)
- [ ] Photos of programs/participants

## Anatomy of a Grant Proposal

### Standard Components

| Section | Length | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Cover Letter** | 1 page | Introduction, ask, fit |
| **Executive Summary** | 1/2-1 page | Entire proposal summarized |
| **Organization Background** | 1-2 pages | Who you are, credibility |
| **Statement of Need** | 1-2 pages | Problem you're addressing |
| **Program Description** | 2-4 pages | What you'll do, how |
| **Goals & Objectives** | 1 page | Measurable outcomes |
| **Evaluation Plan** | 1 page | How you'll measure success |
| **Budget & Narrative** | 1-2 pages | Costs and justification |
| **Sustainability** | 1/2-1 page | How you'll continue after grant |
| **Attachments** | Varies | Supporting documents |

---

## Writing Tips

### General Principles

1. **Follow instructions exactly** - Page limits, font size, required sections
2. **Answer the question asked** - Don't repurpose old proposals blindly
3. **Be specific and concrete** - Numbers, names, dates, places
4. **Show don't tell** - Stories, examples, data
5. **Write clearly** - Short sentences, active voice, no jargon
6. **Proofread** - Errors signal carelessness

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Better Approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| Vague goals | Specific, measurable objectives |
| All about you | Focus on community impact |
| Jargon-heavy | Plain language |
| No evidence of need | Data, quotes, stories |
| Unrealistic budget | Research actual costs |
| No sustainability plan | Show path to ongoing funding |
| Generic proposal | Tailored to funder priorities |
| Missing deadline | Submit 2+ days early |

## Sample Proposal Sections

### Cover Letter Template

```
[Date]

[Funder Name]
[Address]

RE: Grant Application for [Program Name]

Dear [Contact Name or "Grant Review Committee"],

[Organization Name] respectfully requests a grant of $[AMOUNT]
from [Funder Name] to support [brief program description].

[One paragraph about your organization - who you are, what you do,
who you serve, why you're credible]

[One paragraph about the specific project/program - what it is,
who benefits, expected outcomes]

[One paragraph about fit with funder - why this aligns with their
priorities, any previous relationship]

We would welcome the opportunity to discuss this proposal further.
Please contact me at [phone] or [email] with any questions.

Thank you for your consideration and your commitment to [funder's
mission area].

Sincerely,

[Name]
[Title]
[Organization]

Enclosures: [List attachments]
```

### Executive Summary Template

```
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

[Organization Name] requests $[AMOUNT] from [Funder] to [one
sentence description of what grant will fund].

THE NEED: [2-3 sentences describing the problem with supporting
data. Example: "In [City], X% of low-income residents lack
reliable transportation. Public transit is limited, and a
reliable used car costs $X,000—out of reach for families
earning below $X/year."]

OUR SOLUTION: [2-3 sentences describing your program. Example:
"Our Earn-A-Bike program provides free bicycles and repair
training to low-income adults. Participants complete X hours
of classes, learning to maintain their own bikes while earning
a refurbished bicycle, helmet, and lock."]

EXPECTED OUTCOMES: [Bullet list of measurable results]
• [X] adults will complete the program
• [X]% will report using their bike for transportation weekly
• [X] volunteer hours will be contributed
• [X] bikes will be diverted from landfill

BUDGET: The total program cost is $[X]. We are requesting
$[AMOUNT] from [Funder], representing [X]% of the total.
[Other funding sources] will provide the remaining support.

[Organization] has successfully operated since [YEAR], serving
[X] community members annually. This grant will [expand/launch/
strengthen] our capacity to [impact statement].
```

### Statement of Need Template

```
STATEMENT OF NEED

[Opening hook - story, statistic, or quote that captures attention]

THE PROBLEM

[City/Region] faces significant transportation challenges that
disproportionately affect low-income residents.

[Data point 1 with citation]: According to [source], X% of
households in [area] do not have access to a reliable vehicle.

[Data point 2]: Public transportation in [city] is limited,
with [specific limitations - frequency, coverage, hours].

[Data point 3]: The average cost of [car ownership/transit pass]
is $X per [month/year], representing X% of income for a family
at the poverty level.

THE IMPACT

Without reliable transportation, residents struggle to:
• Access employment (cite unemployment data if available)
• Reach healthcare appointments
• Get children to school and activities
• Purchase affordable groceries (food desert data)
• Participate in community life

[Quote from community member or partner organization describing
the problem in human terms]

THE OPPORTUNITY

Bicycles offer a low-cost, healthy, and sustainable transportation
solution. However, many residents:
• Cannot afford to purchase a bike ($X for basic new bike)
• Lack knowledge to maintain a bicycle
• Do not have access to tools or workspace for repairs

[Organization Name]'s programs directly address these barriers
by providing [brief program description].

[Close with transition to your solution]
```

### Program Description Template

```
PROGRAM DESCRIPTION

OVERVIEW

[Program Name] is a [duration]-week program that provides
[target population] with [what they receive/learn]. The program
addresses [need] by [approach].

Since [year], we have served [X] participants, with [outcome
statistic that demonstrates success].

PROGRAM MODEL

[Detailed description of how the program works]

Eligibility: [Who can participate, how they're selected]

Curriculum: [What participants learn/do]
• Week 1: [Topic]
• Week 2: [Topic]
• [Continue as needed]

Staffing: [Who delivers the program - staff, volunteers,
instructors]

Location: [Where program takes place]

Schedule: [When - days, times, frequency]

PARTICIPANTS

We will serve [X] participants through this grant, with the
following target demographics:
• [X]% low-income (below X% AMI)
• [X]% youth ages [X-X]
• [X]% from [specific neighborhood/population]

Participants are recruited through:
• Partner referrals ([list partners])
• Community outreach ([describe methods])
• Word of mouth from past participants

WHAT PARTICIPANTS RECEIVE

Upon completion, each participant receives:
• Refurbished bicycle (valued at $X)
• Helmet and lock
• Basic repair skills training
• Ongoing access to our shop for repairs
• [Other benefits]

PARTNERS

[Organization] collaborates with the following partners to
deliver this program:

[Partner 1]: [Their role]
[Partner 2]: [Their role]

[Include letter of support from key partners as attachment]
```

### Goals & Objectives Template

```
GOALS AND OBJECTIVES

GOAL 1: Increase access to reliable transportation for
low-income residents of [City/Region]

  Objective 1.1: Enroll [X] participants in Earn-A-Bike
  program by [date]

  Objective 1.2: Graduate [X] participants (X% completion
  rate) with refurbished bicycles by [date]

  Objective 1.3: [X]% of graduates report using their
  bicycle for transportation at least weekly at 3-month
  follow-up

GOAL 2: Build community capacity for bicycle repair and
maintenance

  Objective 2.1: Provide [X] hours of repair education
  through classes and open shop

  Objective 2.2: Train [X] new volunteer mechanics

  Objective 2.3: Divert [X] bicycles from landfill through
  refurbishment or parts harvesting

GOAL 3: [Third goal if applicable]

  Objective 3.1: [Measurable objective]
  Objective 3.2: [Measurable objective]
```

### Evaluation Plan Template

```
EVALUATION PLAN

[Organization] is committed to measuring our impact and
continuously improving our programs.

DATA COLLECTION

| Metric | Method | Frequency | Responsible |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Enrollment | Registration forms | Ongoing | Program Coord |
| Completion | Attendance records | End of cohort | Program Coord |
| Skills gained | Pre/post assessment | Start/end | Instructor |
| Bike usage | Follow-up survey | 3 months post | Program Coord |
| Satisfaction | Exit survey | End of cohort | Program Coord |

OUTCOME MEASUREMENT

Output Measures (activities completed):
• Number of participants enrolled
• Number of classes held
• Number of bikes distributed
• Number of volunteer hours

Outcome Measures (changes for participants):
• % completing program
• % demonstrating repair skills proficiency
• % using bike for transportation at 3 months
• % reporting improved access to employment/services

REPORTING

We will provide [Funder] with:
• Interim report at [midpoint] including [metrics]
• Final report within [X] days of grant end including all
  outcome data and narrative summary
• Photos and participant testimonials (with permission)

CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

Program staff meet [monthly/quarterly] to review data and
identify improvements. Participant feedback directly informs
program adjustments.
```

## After the Award

### If Funded

1. **Send thank you** - Immediate acknowledgment
2. **Review requirements** - Reporting, restrictions, timeline
3. **Set up tracking** - Budget codes, outcome tracking
4. **Implement program** - Follow proposed plan
5. **Document everything** - Photos, stories, data
6. **Submit reports on time** - Never late
7. **Communicate proactively** - Alert funder to changes
8. **Invite site visit** - Let them see impact
9. **Acknowledge publicly** - Per funder guidelines

### If Not Funded

1. **Thank them anyway** - Maintain relationship
2. **Request feedback** - Ask what would strengthen future applications
3. **Learn from it** - Improve next proposal
4. **Try again** - Many funders expect multiple applications
5. **Consider smaller ask** - Start with smaller grant to build relationship

### Reporting Best Practices

- Submit on time (or early)
- Follow format requested
- Lead with outcomes and impact
- Include compelling stories (with permission)
- Be honest about challenges
- Show photos of program in action
- Connect spending to impact
- Thank funder in the report

## Sample Letters of Support

### Partner Organization Letter

```
[Partner Letterhead]

[Date]

To Whom It May Concern:

[Partner Organization] is pleased to support [Co-op Name]'s
application to [Funder] for the [Program Name].

[Partner] is a [description of partner org] serving [population]
in [area]. We have partnered with [Co-op] since [year] to
[describe collaboration].

Through this partnership, we have seen [specific positive
outcomes]. For example, [brief story or statistic].

[Co-op]'s programs address a critical need in our community.
Many of the [clients/students/residents] we serve lack reliable
transportation, which creates barriers to [employment/education/
healthcare]. The Earn-A-Bike program provides not just a bicycle,
but skills and confidence that extend beyond transportation.

We strongly support this application and look forward to
continuing our partnership with [Co-op].

Sincerely,

[Name]
[Title]
[Partner Organization]
[Phone/Email]
```

```

I use to [how they use it - commute, get to school, etc.].

The bicycle I earned has [describe impact - saved money, improved
health, got a job, etc.].

I strongly encourage you to fund [Co-op]'s programs. They make a
real difference in people's lives.

Sincerely,

[First Name and Last Initial]
[City, State]
```
