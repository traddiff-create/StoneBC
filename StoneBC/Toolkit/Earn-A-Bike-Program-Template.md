# Earn-A-Bike Program Template

## Overview

An Earn-A-Bike (EAB) program allows participants to earn a bicycle by learning repair skills and contributing volunteer time. This is a cornerstone program for most bicycle cooperatives.

---

## Program Goals

1. **Teach bicycle repair skills** - Participants learn to maintain and repair bicycles
2. **Provide accessible transportation** - People earn bikes regardless of income
3. **Build community** - Participants contribute to the co-op while learning
4. **Develop workforce skills** - Punctuality, tool use, problem-solving, teamwork
5. **Recycle bicycles** - Give donated bikes new life

## Curriculum Outline

### Module 1: Introduction & Safety (Session 1)

**Topics:**
- Welcome and program overview
- Shop tour and rules
- Tool identification and proper use
- Personal protective equipment
- Bicycle anatomy - parts identification

**Hands-On:**
- Tool organization exercise
- Identify parts on a bike

**Assessment:**
- Safety quiz (verbal or written)

---

### Module 2: Flat Repair (Session 2)

**Topics:**
- Types of tires and tubes
- Common causes of flats
- Patch kit vs. tube replacement
- Tire inspection for wear/damage
- Proper inflation

**Hands-On:**
- Remove and reinstall wheel
- Remove tire from rim
- Patch a tube
- Install new tube
- Check tire seating and inflation

**Assessment:**
- Successfully patch and reinstall a tube

---

### Module 3: Brakes (Session 3)

**Topics:**
- Brake types (rim, disc, coaster)
- Brake cable and housing
- Pad inspection and replacement
- Adjustment procedures

**Hands-On:**
- Adjust rim brakes (caliper or V-brake)
- Replace brake pads
- Adjust cable tension
- Center brakes

**Assessment:**
- Properly adjusted, functional brakes

---

### Module 4: Drivetrain (Session 4)

**Topics:**
- Chain, chainrings, cassette/freewheel
- Derailleurs (front and rear)
- Shifters and cables
- Cleaning and lubrication

**Hands-On:**
- Clean and lube a chain
- Adjust rear derailleur limit screws
- Adjust cable tension
- Check chain wear

**Assessment:**
- Smooth shifting through all gears

---

### Module 5: Wheels (Session 5)

**Topics:**
- Hub types and bearings
- Spoke tension and wheel truing
- Quick release vs. bolt-on
- Wheel inspection

**Hands-On:**
- True a wheel (basic)
- Adjust hub bearings (if applicable)
- Check spoke tension

**Assessment:**
- Wheel runs true within tolerance

---

### Module 6: Final Check & Graduation (Session 6)

**Topics:**
- ABC Quick Check (Air, Brakes, Chain, Quick releases)
- Pre-ride safety inspection
- Basic maintenance schedule
- Road rules and safety

**Hands-On:**
- Complete safety check on their bike
- Make final adjustments
- Test ride

**Graduation:**
- Certificate of completion
- Receive bike, helmet, lock
- Photo opportunity
- Join as co-op member (optional)

## Bike Selection

### Options for Bike Assignment

**Option A: Assigned Bike**
- Participant works on assigned bike throughout program
- Earns that specific bike upon completion

**Option B: Choice at Graduation**
- Participant works on various shop bikes
- Chooses from available bikes at graduation

**Option C: Build-A-Bike**
- Advanced option for longer programs
- Participant builds bike from frame up

### Bike Standards

All earned bikes must meet minimum standards:
- [ ] Frame - no cracks, proper size for rider
- [ ] Brakes - functional front and rear
- [ ] Wheels - true, properly tensioned, good tires
- [ ] Drivetrain - shifts smoothly, chain in good condition
- [ ] Headset/stem - secure, turns freely
- [ ] Seatpost/saddle - secure, appropriate height
- [ ] Reflectors/lights - as required by law

## Program Variations

### Youth EAB
- Ages 10-17
- Shorter sessions (1.5-2 hours)
- More hands-on, less lecture
- Parent involvement encouraged
- Additional focus on road safety

### Women/Trans/Femme EAB
- Creates safer space for underserved populations
- Same curriculum, designated sessions
- Women/trans/femme instructors when possible

### Advanced EAB
- For participants who completed basic program
- Wheel building, hub overhaul, headset service
- Opportunity to earn higher-end bikes

### Family EAB
- Parent and child participate together
- Each earns a bike
- Builds family cycling culture

---

## Instructor Guidelines

### Qualifications
- Complete instructor training
- Demonstrate proficiency in all curriculum areas
- Background check (if working with youth)
- First aid/CPR certification (recommended)

### Teaching Tips
- Demonstrate, then let participants try
- Patience - everyone learns differently
- Encourage questions
- Celebrate small victories
- Focus on safety always

### Instructor-to-Participant Ratio
- Recommended: 1:4 to 1:6
- For youth programs: 1:3 to 1:4
