# Bicycle Cooperative Bylaws Template

> **Instructions:** This template provides a starting framework for bicycle cooperative bylaws. Replace all `[BRACKETED TEXT]` with your organization's specific information. Consult with a local attorney to ensure compliance with your state's laws.

---

## BYLAWS OF [COOPERATIVE NAME]

A Cooperative Corporation organized under the laws of the State of [STATE]

**Adopted: [DATE]**

---

## ARTICLE I: NAME AND PURPOSE

### Section 1.1 - Name
The name of this cooperative shall be **[COOPERATIVE NAME]**, hereinafter referred to as "the Cooperative."

### Section 1.2 - Purpose
The purposes for which this Cooperative is organized are:

1. To operate a community bicycle cooperative providing bicycle repair education, tools, workspace, and resources to members and the community
2. To promote cycling as sustainable transportation and recreation
3. To make bicycle ownership and repair accessible to all community members regardless of income
4. To operate earn-a-bike and youth development programs
5. To advocate for cycling infrastructure and safety in [CITY/REGION]
6. To engage in any lawful activity for which cooperatives may be organized under the laws of [STATE]

### Section 1.3 - Nonprofit Operation
The Cooperative shall be operated on a nonprofit basis for the mutual benefit of its members. Net margins, if any, shall be allocated to members based on patronage or retained for cooperative purposes.

---

## ARTICLE II: MEMBERSHIP

### Section 2.1 - Eligibility
Membership shall be open to any person who:
1. Is at least [16/18] years of age (or has parental consent if under 18)
2. Supports the purposes of the Cooperative
3. Pays the required membership fee
4. Agrees to abide by these Bylaws and Cooperative policies

### Section 2.2 - Membership Classes
The Cooperative shall have the following membership classes:

**A. Regular Members**
- Annual fee: $[AMOUNT]
- Full voting rights
- Access to shop during open hours
- Discounts on parts and services

**B. Volunteer Members**
- Earned through [X] hours of volunteer service
- Full voting rights
- Access to shop during open hours
- Discounts on parts and services

**C. Youth Members (under 18)**
- Annual fee: $[AMOUNT] (reduced)
- Non-voting (may attend meetings)
- Access to shop with adult supervision
- Participation in youth programs

**D. Supporting Members**
- Annual fee: $[AMOUNT] or more
- Full voting rights
- Recognition as cooperative supporter
- All Regular Member benefits

### Section 2.3 - Application Process
1. Complete membership application
2. Pay applicable fee (or complete volunteer hours)
3. Attend new member orientation
4. Receive membership card/certificate

### Section 2.4 - Rights of Members
Each member in good standing shall have the right to:
1. One vote on all matters submitted to members
2. Attend and participate in member meetings
3. Access cooperative facilities during designated hours
4. Run for election to the Board of Directors
5. Inspect cooperative records upon reasonable request
6. Receive notice of meetings and cooperative activities

### Section 2.5 - Responsibilities of Members
Members agree to:
1. Support the mission and purpose of the Cooperative
2. Follow all safety rules and policies
3. Treat all members, volunteers, and guests with respect
4. Pay dues on time
5. Contribute volunteer time when able

### Section 2.6 - Termination of Membership
Membership may be terminated by:
1. **Voluntary withdrawal** - Written notice to the Secretary
2. **Non-payment** - Failure to pay dues within [60] days of due date
3. **Expulsion** - For cause, by two-thirds vote of the Board after opportunity to be heard

### Section 2.7 - Transfer of Membership
Membership is not transferable.

---

## ARTICLE III: MEETINGS OF MEMBERS

### Section 3.1 - Annual Meeting
The annual meeting of members shall be held in [MONTH] of each year at a time and place determined by the Board of Directors. The purpose of the annual meeting shall be to:
1. Elect Directors
2. Receive annual reports
3. Conduct other business as needed

### Section 3.2 - Special Meetings
Special meetings may be called by:
1. The Board of Directors
2. The President
3. Written petition of [10%/25] or more members

### Section 3.3 - Notice
Written notice of all member meetings shall be provided at least [14/21/30] days in advance, stating the date, time, place, and purpose of the meeting. Notice may be provided by:
- Email to members' registered email address
- Posting at the cooperative location
- Mail to members' registered address

### Section 3.4 - Quorum
A quorum for the transaction of business at any member meeting shall be [10%/15/25] members present in person or by proxy.

### Section 3.5 - Voting
1. Each member shall have one vote
2. Voting may be in person or by written proxy
3. Decisions shall be by majority vote unless otherwise specified
4. Voting by electronic means may be permitted as approved by the Board

### Section 3.6 - Action Without Meeting
Any action that may be taken at a member meeting may be taken without a meeting if written consent is obtained from [majority/two-thirds] of all members entitled to vote.

---

## ARTICLE IV: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 4.1 - Powers and Duties
The Board of Directors shall manage the affairs of the Cooperative and shall have all powers necessary to carry out the purposes of the Cooperative, including but not limited to:
1. Setting policies and strategic direction
2. Hiring and supervising staff (if any)
3. Approving annual budget
4. Ensuring financial accountability
5. Maintaining cooperative property
6. Admitting and expelling members

### Section 4.2 - Composition
The Board shall consist of [5/7/9] Directors, all of whom must be members in good standing.

### Section 4.3 - Election and Term
1. Directors shall be elected by the members at the annual meeting
2. Terms shall be [2/3] years
3. Terms shall be staggered so approximately [one-third/one-half] of Directors are elected each year
4. Directors may serve no more than [2/3] consecutive terms

### Section 4.4 - Vacancies
Vacancies on the Board may be filled by majority vote of the remaining Directors. A Director appointed to fill a vacancy shall serve until the next annual meeting.

### Section 4.5 - Removal
A Director may be removed with or without cause by two-thirds vote of the members at a meeting called for that purpose.

### Section 4.6 - Compensation
Directors shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in performance of their duties.

---

## ARTICLE V: MEETINGS OF THE BOARD

### Section 5.1 - Regular Meetings
The Board shall meet at least [monthly/quarterly] at a time and place determined by the Board.

### Section 5.2 - Special Meetings
Special meetings may be called by the President or any [2/3] Directors with [48 hours/7 days] notice.

### Section 5.3 - Notice
Notice of Board meetings shall be given at least [7] days in advance by email or other reasonable means.

### Section 5.4 - Quorum
A quorum shall be a majority of the Directors then in office.

### Section 5.5 - Voting
Each Director shall have one vote. Decisions shall be by majority vote of Directors present, except as otherwise provided in these Bylaws.

### Section 5.6 - Open Meetings
Board meetings shall be open to all members except when the Board enters executive session for personnel, legal, or sensitive financial matters.

### Section 5.7 - Action Without Meeting
The Board may act without a meeting if all Directors consent in writing to the action taken.

### Section 5.8 - Remote Participation
Directors may participate in meetings by telephone or video conference and shall be considered present for quorum and voting purposes.

---

## ARTICLE VI: OFFICERS

### Section 6.1 - Officers
The officers of the Cooperative shall be:
1. President
2. Vice President
3. Secretary
4. Treasurer

The Board may create additional officer positions as needed.

### Section 6.2 - Election and Term
Officers shall be elected by the Board from among the Directors at the first Board meeting following the annual member meeting. Officers shall serve for one year or until their successors are elected.

### Section 6.3 - President
The President shall:
1. Preside at all member and Board meetings
2. Serve as chief executive officer
3. Sign documents on behalf of the Cooperative
4. Appoint committee chairs (with Board approval)
5. Perform other duties as assigned by the Board

### Section 6.4 - Vice President
The Vice President shall:
1. Perform duties of President in their absence
2. Assist President as requested
3. Perform other duties as assigned by the Board

### Section 6.5 - Secretary
The Secretary shall:
1. Keep minutes of all meetings
2. Maintain membership records
3. Provide notice of meetings
4. Maintain corporate records and documents
5. Perform other duties as assigned by the Board

### Section 6.6 - Treasurer
The Treasurer shall:
1. Oversee financial records and accounts
2. Provide financial reports to the Board
3. Ensure timely filing of tax returns
4. Maintain adequate insurance
5. Perform other duties as assigned by the Board

### Section 6.7 - Removal
Any officer may be removed by majority vote of the Board at any time.

---

## ARTICLE VII: COMMITTEES

### Section 7.1 - Standing Committees
The Board may establish standing committees including but not limited to:
1. **Finance Committee** - Budget, financial oversight
2. **Membership Committee** - Recruitment, orientation, engagement
3. **Programs Committee** - Earn-a-bike, youth programs, education
4. **Facilities Committee** - Shop maintenance, tools, safety
5. **Fundraising Committee** - Grants, donations, events

### Section 7.2 - Ad Hoc Committees
The Board may establish ad hoc committees for specific purposes as needed.

### Section 7.3 - Committee Authority
Committees may make recommendations to the Board but shall not have authority to act on behalf of the Cooperative unless specifically delegated by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: FINANCES

### Section 8.1 - Fiscal Year
The fiscal year shall be [January 1 - December 31 / July 1 - June 30].

### Section 8.2 - Budget
The Board shall adopt an annual budget prior to the beginning of each fiscal year.

### Section 8.3 - Banking
All funds shall be deposited in accounts at financial institutions approved by the Board. Withdrawals require [one/two] authorized signatures for amounts over $[500/1000].

### Section 8.4 - Financial Review
The Board shall arrange for an annual financial review or audit as appropriate to the size of the Cooperative.

### Section 8.5 - Patronage Allocation
Net margins, if any, shall be allocated to members based on their patronage of the Cooperative during the fiscal year. Allocation and distribution shall be determined by the Board in accordance with state law.

---

## ARTICLE IX: INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 9.1 - Indemnification
The Cooperative shall indemnify any Director, officer, or agent against expenses, judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred in connection with any action, suit, or proceeding if such person acted in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Cooperative.

---

## ARTICLE X: AMENDMENTS

### Section 10.1 - Proposal
Amendments to these Bylaws may be proposed by:
1. The Board of Directors
2. Written petition of [10%/25] or more members

### Section 10.2 - Notice
Proposed amendments shall be provided to all members at least [21/30] days before the meeting at which they will be considered.

### Section 10.3 - Adoption
Amendments shall be adopted by [two-thirds vote of members present at a meeting / majority vote of all members].

---

## ARTICLE XI: DISSOLUTION

### Section 11.1 - Dissolution
The Cooperative may be dissolved by two-thirds vote of all members at a meeting called for that purpose.

### Section 11.2 - Distribution of Assets
Upon dissolution, after paying all debts and obligations, remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations with similar purposes, as determined by the Board, or as required by law.
