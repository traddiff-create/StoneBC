# South Dakota Cooperative Law Guide

## Overview

South Dakota cooperative law is governed by **SDCL Title 47, Chapters 15-20**. This guide summarizes the key requirements for forming and operating a cooperative in South Dakota.

---

## Relevant Statutes

| Chapter | Title | Coverage |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **47-15** | Cooperatives--Formation And General Powers | Articles of incorporation, formation requirements |
| **47-16** | Membership, Stock And Distributions | Member rights, voting, patronage |
| **47-17** | Directors, Officers And Employees | Governance structure |
| **47-18** | Reorganization And Dissolution | Winding up, merger |
| **47-19** | Foreign Cooperatives | Out-of-state cooperatives |
| **47-20** | Records, Accounts And Reports | Recordkeeping requirements |

---

## Formation Requirements (SDCL 47-15)

### Who Can Form
- Five or more natural persons may form a cooperative
- Existing corporations may convert to cooperative status

### Articles of Incorporation Must Include
1. **Name** - Must comply with SDCL 47-15-42 naming requirements
2. **Purpose** - Statement of cooperative's purpose
3. **Principal Office** - Location in South Dakota
4. **Duration** - May be perpetual or fixed term
5. **Incorporators** - Names and addresses of at least 5 incorporators
6. **Capital Structure** - Description of membership certificates or stock
7. **Registered Agent** - Name and address in South Dakota

### Filing Process
1. Prepare Articles of Incorporation
2. File with South Dakota Secretary of State
3. Pay filing fee (check current fee at sdsos.gov)
4. Receive Certificate of Incorporation

---

## Converting to Cooperative (SDCL 47-15-14)

An existing corporation (including LLC) may convert to a cooperative by:

1. **Adopting an amendment** to articles electing to become subject to chapters 47-15 through 47-20
2. **Making required changes** to articles as specified by cooperative statutes
3. **Filing the amendment** per applicable corporate law
4. **Conversion effective** upon issuance of certificate of amendment by Secretary of State

> **Note for Stone Bicycle Coalition:** This provides a pathway to convert the current LLC structure to a true cooperative without dissolving the existing entity.

## Membership Requirements (SDCL 47-16)

### Membership Certificates
- Cooperative must issue membership certificates
- One vote per member (regardless of shares held)
- Members must meet qualifications set in bylaws

### Voting Rights
- Democratic principle: one member, one vote
- Proxy voting may be allowed per bylaws
- Quorum requirements set in bylaws

### Patronage Distributions
- Net margins may be distributed based on patronage (use of cooperative services)
- Not based on capital contribution

---

## Governance (SDCL 47-17)

### Board of Directors
- Minimum 3 directors
- Elected by members
- Terms set in bylaws (typically 1-3 years)
- Staggered terms recommended for continuity

### Officers
- President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer (minimum)
- May have additional officers per bylaws
- Officers elected by board or members per bylaws

### Meetings
- Annual member meeting required
- Board meetings as specified in bylaws
- Notice requirements per bylaws (typically 10-30 days)

---

## Recordkeeping (SDCL 47-20)

### Required Records
- Articles of Incorporation and amendments
- Bylaws and amendments
- Minutes of member and board meetings
- Member register (names, addresses, certificates)
- Financial records and annual statements

### Member Access
- Members have right to inspect records
- Reasonable time and place
- May charge reasonable copying fees

## Pathway for Stone Bicycle Coalition

### Current Structure
- LLC (Manager-Managed)
- Partnership taxation (Form 1065)

### Recommended Path to Cooperative

**Option A: Convert LLC to Cooperative**
1. Research if SD allows direct LLC-to-cooperative conversion
2. If yes, file amendment per SDCL 47-15-14 process
3. Adopt cooperative bylaws
4. Issue membership certificates

**Option B: Form New Cooperative, Transfer Assets**
1. Form new cooperative corporation under SDCL 47-15
2. Transfer LLC assets to cooperative
3. Dissolve LLC
4. More complex but cleaner structure

**Option C: Hybrid Model**
1. Maintain LLC for certain functions (property holding)
2. Form cooperative for member-facing operations
3. Operating agreement between entities

### Recommended Steps
1. [ ] Consult with SD business attorney familiar with cooperatives
2. [ ] Determine best conversion pathway
3. [ ] Draft cooperative bylaws
4. [ ] Prepare articles of incorporation/amendment
5. [ ] Hold organizational meeting
6. [ ] File with Secretary of State
7. [ ] Update IRS classification (Form 8832 if needed)
