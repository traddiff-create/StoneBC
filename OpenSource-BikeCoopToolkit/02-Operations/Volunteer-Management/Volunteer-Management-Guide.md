# Volunteer Management Guide

## Overview

Volunteers are the lifeblood of most bicycle cooperatives. This guide covers recruitment, training, scheduling, retention, and recognition of volunteers.

---

## Volunteer Program Goals

1. **Build community** - Create meaningful connections through shared work
2. **Develop skills** - Train volunteers in bicycle repair and leadership
3. **Sustain operations** - Maintain consistent shop hours and programs
4. **Create pathways** - Opportunities for growth from volunteer to leader
5. **Share ownership** - Volunteers invested in co-op success

---

## Volunteer Roles

### Tier 1: Entry Level (0-20 hours)

| Role | Description | Training Required |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Shop Helper** | General assistance, cleaning, organizing | Orientation only |
| **Greeter** | Welcome visitors, sign-in, basic info | Orientation only |
| **Bike Receiver** | Accept donations, initial sorting | 2-hour training |
| **Parts Sorter** | Organize and label parts inventory | 2-hour training |

### Tier 2: Trained (20-50 hours)

| Role | Description | Training Required |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Shop Volunteer** | Assist visitors with repairs, answer questions | 8-hour training |
| **Mechanic Assistant** | Support lead mechanics, learn advanced repairs | 16-hour training |
| **Program Assistant** | Help with classes, youth programs | Program-specific |
| **Outreach Helper** | Community events, tabling, promotion | 2-hour training |

### Tier 3: Leadership (50+ hours)

| Role | Description | Training Required |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Shift Lead** | Open/close shop, supervise volunteers, handle issues | 24+ hours + certification |
| **Lead Mechanic** | Advanced repairs, train others, quality control | 40+ hours + assessment |
| **Program Lead** | Run classes, develop curriculum | Program certification |
| **Volunteer Coordinator** | Recruit, schedule, support volunteers | Leadership training |

### Tier 4: Leadership Team

| Role | Description | Selection |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Shop Manager** | Overall operations, staff supervision | Hired or elected |
| **Program Director** | All programs, curriculum, partnerships | Hired or elected |
| **Board Member** | Governance, strategy, fiduciary duty | Elected by members |

## Onboarding Process

### Step 1: Application

Collect basic information:
- Name, contact info, emergency contact
- Availability (days/times)
- Interests and skills
- How they heard about the co-op
- Background check consent (if required for youth programs)

### Step 2: Orientation (2 hours)

**Agenda:**
1. Welcome and introductions (15 min)
2. Co-op mission and history (15 min)
3. Shop tour and layout (20 min)
4. Safety rules and procedures (20 min)
5. Volunteer roles overview (15 min)
6. Policies and expectations (15 min)
7. Paperwork and questions (20 min)

**Required Paperwork:**
- [ ] Volunteer agreement signed
- [ ] Liability waiver signed
- [ ] Emergency contact form
- [ ] Photo release (optional)
- [ ] Background check (if applicable)

### Step 3: Shadow Shifts (2-4 hours)

New volunteers shadow experienced volunteers for:
- Learning shop flow and routines
- Meeting other volunteers
- Asking questions in real situations
- Assessing fit and interest

### Step 4: Independent Volunteering

After orientation + shadow shifts:
- Assigned to regular shift or on-call list
- Paired with mentor for first month
- Check-ins at 2 weeks and 1 month

## Training Programs

### Basic Shop Training (8 hours)

**Module 1: Shop Operations (2 hours)**
- Opening/closing procedures
- Point of sale / cash handling
- Visitor sign-in and assistance
- When to ask for help

**Module 2: Safety & Tools (2 hours)**
- Personal protective equipment
- Tool identification and use
- Hazardous materials handling
- Injury and incident reporting

**Module 3: Basic Repairs (4 hours)**
- Flat repair (tube, tire, patch)
- Brake adjustment
- Derailleur adjustment
- Chain lubrication
- ABC Quick Check

### Advanced Mechanic Training (16+ hours)

**Topics covered:**
- Wheel truing and spoke replacement
- Hub overhaul and bearing adjustment
- Bottom bracket service
- Headset service
- Cable and housing replacement
- Brake system overhaul
- Drivetrain diagnosis

**Assessment:**
- Practical skills test
- Teaching demonstration
- Supervisor sign-off

### Shift Lead Training (8 hours)

**Topics covered:**
- Opening and closing procedures
- Cash handling and reconciliation
- Conflict resolution
- Emergency procedures
- Volunteer supervision
- Decision-making authority
- Incident reporting

**Requirements:**
- 50+ volunteer hours
- Basic shop training complete
- Recommendation from current shift lead
- Trial shifts under supervision

## Scheduling

### Scheduling Models

**Model A: Fixed Shifts**
- Volunteers commit to same shift weekly
- Predictable, builds routine
- Requires backup system for absences

**Model B: Sign-Up System**
- Volunteers sign up for available shifts
- Flexible, accommodates varying schedules
- May have gaps in coverage

**Model C: Hybrid**
- Core volunteers on fixed shifts
- Additional slots open for sign-up
- Best of both approaches

## Policies

### Attendance Policy

**Expectations:**
- Confirm shifts at least 48 hours in advance
- Notify of absence ASAP (call/text, don't just no-show)
- Find replacement if possible, or notify shift lead
- Arrive on time (within 10 minutes of shift start)

**Consequences:**
- 1st no-show: Verbal reminder
- 2nd no-show: Written warning
- 3rd no-show: Meeting with coordinator
- Pattern of no-shows: May be removed from schedule

### Code of Conduct

All volunteers agree to:

1. **Respect** - Treat everyone with dignity regardless of background
2. **Safety** - Follow all safety rules, report hazards
3. **Honesty** - Be truthful, don't take what isn't yours
4. **Inclusion** - Welcome all community members
5. **Responsibility** - Complete tasks, ask for help when needed
6. **Confidentiality** - Protect member/visitor privacy
7. **Sobriety** - No alcohol or drugs during volunteer time
8. **Representation** - Represent the co-op positively

### Conflict Resolution

**Step 1: Direct conversation**
- Address issue directly with the person if safe
- Use "I" statements, focus on behavior not person

**Step 2: Shift lead mediation**
- If direct conversation doesn't resolve, involve shift lead
- Shift lead facilitates conversation

**Step 3: Coordinator involvement**
- For serious or ongoing issues
- May involve temporary separation

**Step 4: Formal process**
- Written complaint
- Investigation
- Possible consequences up to removal

### Grounds for Immediate Removal

- Violence or threats of violence
- Theft
- Harassment or discrimination
- Intoxication during volunteer time
- Serious safety violations
- Illegal activity on premises

## Retention Strategies

### Why Volunteers Leave

| Reason | Prevention Strategy |
|--------|---------------------|
| Burnout | Reasonable expectations, time off |
| Feeling unappreciated | Regular recognition, thank-yous |
| Lack of growth | Training opportunities, new roles |
| Poor fit | Better matching during onboarding |
| Life changes | Flexible scheduling, leave options |
| Conflict | Address issues promptly, mediation |
| Boredom | Variety of tasks, new challenges |

### Retention Best Practices

1. **Say thank you** - Frequently and specifically
2. **Check in regularly** - Ask how they're doing
3. **Offer growth** - Training, new responsibilities
4. **Create community** - Social events, shared meals
5. **Listen to feedback** - Act on suggestions when possible
6. **Respect time** - Start and end on time
7. **Provide support** - Tools, training, backup
8. **Celebrate milestones** - Hours, anniversaries, achievements

### Volunteer Leave Policy

Volunteers may take leave for:
- Vacation / travel
- Health issues
- Family obligations
- School demands
- Burnout recovery

**Process:**
1. Notify coordinator of leave dates
2. No minimum/maximum leave length
3. Welcome back when ready
4. May need refresher training after extended leave

## Recognition Program

### Informal Recognition

- **Daily:** Verbal thank-yous, specific praise
- **Weekly:** Shout-outs at shift change, in communications
- **Monthly:** Social media features, newsletter mentions

### Formal Recognition

| Milestone | Recognition |
|-----------|-------------|
| 25 hours | Thank-you card, small gift |
| 50 hours | Co-op t-shirt or merchandise |
| 100 hours | Name on "Century Club" board, party |
| 250 hours | Special gift, feature story |
| 500 hours | Lifetime membership, major recognition |
| 1000 hours | "Founder" status, permanent recognition |

### Annual Recognition

- **Volunteer appreciation event** - Annual party/dinner
- **Awards** - Volunteer of the year, rising star, etc.
- **Certificates** - Formal recognition of service
- **Letters** - For resumes, college applications

### Non-Monetary Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Free membership | Shop access, discounts |
| Parts discount | 10-25% off parts |
| First pick | New donated bikes/parts |
| Free classes | Training and workshops |
| Reference letters | For jobs, school |
| Skill development | Repair, leadership, teaching |
| Community | Friends, belonging, purpose |

## Tracking & Reporting

### What to Track

**Per Volunteer:**
- Contact information
- Hours worked (by date and role)
- Training completed
- Roles/certifications
- Recognition given
- Notes/feedback

**Program-Wide:**
- Total volunteer hours (monthly, yearly)
- Number of active volunteers
- Retention rate
- Hours by role/program
- New volunteer pipeline

### Tracking Methods

| Method | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Paper sign-in** | Simple, no tech needed | Manual data entry |
| **Spreadsheet** | Free, customizable | Manual updates |
| **Google Forms** | Easy sign-in, auto-logs | Limited reporting |
| **Volunteer software** | Full features, reporting | Cost, learning curve |

## Special Situations

### Court-Ordered Community Service

**Accepting CS volunteers:**
- Verify paperwork with referring agency
- Treat same as other volunteers
- Track hours carefully, sign forms
- Report any issues to referring agency

**Best practices:**
- Assign entry-level tasks initially
- Pair with regular volunteer
- Some may become long-term volunteers
- Don't assume anything about their situation

### Student Interns

**Structure:**
- Learning objectives defined
- Supervisor assigned
- Regular check-ins
- Final evaluation/report

**Academic credit:**
- Work with school's requirements
- May need formal agreement
- Supervisor provides evaluation

### Youth Volunteers (Under 18)

**Requirements:**
- Parent/guardian consent
- Age-appropriate tasks
- Adult supervision at all times
- Background-checked adults
- Limited hours per labor laws

**Best roles:**
- Parts sorting
- Cleaning
- Assisting with supervision
- Peer teaching (EAB graduates)

## Volunteer Coordinator Role

### Responsibilities

- Recruit new volunteers
- Conduct orientations
- Maintain volunteer database
- Create and manage schedule
- Check in with volunteers regularly
- Address concerns and conflicts
- Coordinate training
- Plan recognition events
- Report to leadership
- Continuously improve program

### Time Commitment

| Co-op Size | Coordinator Hours/Week |
|------------|----------------------|
| Small (<20 volunteers) | 5-10 hours |
| Medium (20-50 volunteers) | 10-20 hours |
| Large (50+ volunteers) | 20-40 hours (often paid) |

### Skills Needed

- Organization and attention to detail
- Communication (written and verbal)
- Conflict resolution
- Empathy and patience
- Basic computer skills
- Knowledge of co-op operations
