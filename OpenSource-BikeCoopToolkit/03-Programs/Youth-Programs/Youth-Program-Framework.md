# Youth Program Framework

## Overview

Youth programs are a cornerstone of bicycle cooperatives, providing young people with mechanical skills, life skills, and a sense of accomplishment. This framework covers program design, safety requirements, curriculum, and best practices.

---

## Why Youth Programs?

### Benefits for Youth

| Area | Benefits |
|------|----------|
| **Mechanical Skills** | Tool use, problem-solving, hands-on learning |
| **Life Skills** | Responsibility, punctuality, follow-through |
| **Physical Health** | Active transportation, outdoor activity |
| **Mental Health** | Accomplishment, confidence, stress relief |
| **Social Skills** | Teamwork, communication, mentorship |
| **Economic** | Free transportation, potential job skills |

### Benefits for Community

- Keeps young people engaged productively
- Creates future cyclists and advocates
- Builds intergenerational connections
- Develops future volunteers and leaders
- Reduces barriers to youth transportation

## Program Types

### 1. Youth Earn-A-Bike

**Ages:** 10-17
**Duration:** 6-8 weeks
**Outcome:** Participant earns a bike, helmet, and lock

Core program where youth learn repair skills and earn their own bicycle. See Earn-A-Bike template for full curriculum - this section covers youth-specific adaptations.

### 2. Youth Mechanic Training

**Ages:** 14-18
**Duration:** Ongoing
**Outcome:** Advanced skills, potential employment

Intensive program for youth who complete Earn-A-Bike and want to develop professional-level skills.

### 3. Summer Bike Camp

**Ages:** 8-14
**Duration:** 1-2 weeks
**Outcome:** Basic skills, safety knowledge, fun

Day camp format combining repair basics, safety education, and group rides.

### 4. After-School Program

**Ages:** 10-16
**Duration:** School year
**Outcome:** Ongoing engagement, skill development

Regular weekly sessions during the school year.

### 5. Drop-In Youth Hours

**Ages:** 10-17
**Duration:** Ongoing
**Outcome:** Accessible entry point

Open shop hours specifically for youth with appropriate supervision.

## Safety & Legal Requirements

### Adult-to-Youth Ratios

| Setting | Minimum Ratio | Recommended |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Shop work | 1:6 | 1:4 |
| Classroom | 1:10 | 1:8 |
| Group rides | 1:5 | 1:4 |
| Off-site events | 1:4 | 1:3 |

*At least 2 adults present at all times*

### Background Checks

All adults working with youth must have:
- [ ] Criminal background check (state + national)
- [ ] Sex offender registry check
- [ ] Reference checks (2-3 references)
- [ ] Application on file

**Frequency:** Initial check + every 2-3 years

**Resources:**
- State Bureau of Investigation
- FBI fingerprint check (if required)
- National Sex Offender Public Website
- Third-party services (Sterling, Checkr, etc.)

### Required Documentation

**Per Participant:**
- [ ] Registration form with emergency contacts
- [ ] Parent/guardian consent form
- [ ] Medical information and allergies
- [ ] Photo release (optional)
- [ ] Liability waiver (signed by parent/guardian)
- [ ] Transportation consent (if applicable)

**Program Records:**
- [ ] Attendance logs
- [ ] Incident reports
- [ ] Staff/volunteer background check records
- [ ] Training documentation

### Supervision Requirements

1. **Two-Adult Rule:** Never one adult alone with youth
2. **Line of Sight:** Youth visible to staff at all times in shop
3. **Bathroom Policy:** Define appropriate procedures
4. **Transportation:** Only pre-approved drivers, parental consent required
5. **Physical Contact:** Appropriate touch policy (e.g., high-fives OK, hugs initiated by youth only)
6. **Communication:** No private social media/texting with youth

### Shop Safety for Youth

**Additional precautions:**
- Mandatory safety glasses for all youth
- Closed-toe shoes required
- Long hair tied back
- No loose clothing
- Direct supervision for all power tools
- Age restrictions on certain tools/tasks
- Smaller tools available for smaller hands

**Tool Restrictions by Age:**

| Tool/Task | Ages 10-12 | Ages 13-15 | Ages 16-17 |
|-----------|------------|------------|------------|
| Hand tools | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Tire levers | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Floor pump | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Cable cutters | Supervised | Yes | Yes |
| Bench grinder | No | Supervised | Supervised |
| Power drill | No | Supervised | Yes |
| Chemicals/degreasers | Supervised | Supervised | Yes |

## Youth Earn-A-Bike Adaptations

### Curriculum Modifications

**Compared to adult program:**
- Shorter sessions (1.5-2 hours vs. 2-3 hours)
- More hands-on, less lecture
- More repetition and review
- Visual aids and demonstrations
- Gamification elements
- Frequent breaks
- Snacks (check allergies!)

### Session Structure (Youth)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0:00 | Welcome, check-in, snack |
| 0:15 | Review last session (quiz game) |
| 0:25 | New skill demonstration |
| 0:35 | Hands-on practice |
| 1:00 | Break / movement activity |
| 1:10 | Continue practice |
| 1:35 | Clean up |
| 1:45 | Recap, preview next session |
| 1:55 | Dismissal (to parents) |

### Engagement Strategies

**Make it fun:**
- Competitions (fastest flat fix, etc.)
- Achievements/badges for skills
- Group challenges
- Music during work time
- Celebrate completions loudly
- Field trips (bike shop, trail ride)

**Build community:**
- Learn everyone's names
- Team names for cohorts
- Group photos
- Graduation ceremony
- Alumni events

**Maintain interest:**
- Variety in activities
- Choice when possible
- Leadership opportunities for advanced youth
- Guest speakers (pro mechanics, racers)

### Behavior Management

**Expectations:**
- Respect for people, tools, and space
- Follow safety rules
- Participate and try your best
- Support your peers

**Positive reinforcement:**
- Specific praise ("Great job centering those brakes!")
- Recognition in front of group
- Extra privileges for good behavior
- Certificates and achievements

**Addressing issues:**
- Redirect before correct
- Private conversations for problems
- Clear, consistent consequences
- Parent communication when needed
- Removal from session as last resort

## Summer Bike Camp

### Sample Schedule (1-Week Camp)

**Daily Schedule:**
| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 9:00 AM | Arrival, free play |
| 9:15 AM | Morning meeting, day overview |
| 9:30 AM | Skill session #1 |
| 10:30 AM | Snack break |
| 10:45 AM | Skill session #2 |
| 11:45 AM | Lunch |
| 12:30 PM | Group ride or outdoor activity |
| 2:00 PM | Skill session #3 or free work time |
| 3:00 PM | Clean up, afternoon snack |
| 3:30 PM | Wrap-up, pickup |

**Weekly Progression:**

| Day | Theme | Skills | Ride |
|-----|-------|--------|------|
| Monday | Bike Anatomy | Parts ID, ABC check | Neighborhood ride |
| Tuesday | Flat Repair | Tubes, patches, tires | Park ride |
| Wednesday | Brakes | Adjustment, pads | Trail ride |
| Thursday | Drivetrain | Chain, gears, shifting | Longer ride |
| Friday | Graduation | Final check, celebration | Group ride to ice cream |

### Camp Logistics

**Registration includes:**
- Emergency contacts (2 minimum)
- Medical info and medications
- Allergies (food and other)
- Photo release
- T-shirt size
- Bike info (bringing own or using provided)

**What to bring daily:**
- Bike and helmet (or use provided)
- Closed-toe shoes
- Weather-appropriate clothing
- Water bottle
- Sunscreen (applied before arrival)
- Lunch and snacks (or provided)

**Staff needed (20 campers):**
- 1 Camp Director
- 3-4 Counselors/instructors
- 1-2 Assistants or junior counselors

## After-School Program

### Structure Options

**Option A: Weekly Sessions**
- Same day each week (e.g., Wednesdays 3:30-5:30)
- Progressive curriculum over semester
- Same cohort throughout

**Option B: Drop-In**
- Multiple days available
- Youth attend when able
- Flexible, skill-based progression

**Option C: Hybrid**
- Core group on set day
- Additional drop-in hours available

### School Partnerships

**Benefits of partnering with schools:**
- Built-in recruitment
- Transportation may be provided
- Academic credit possible
- Access to school facilities
- Referrals from counselors

**Partnership elements:**
- MOU (Memorandum of Understanding)
- Liability and insurance requirements
- Background check compliance
- Communication protocols
- Transportation arrangements
- Academic integration (if applicable)

1. Purpose and goals of partnership
2. Roles and responsibilities
3. Student recruitment and selection
4. Transportation
5. Liability and insurance
6. Background checks
7. Communication and reporting
8. Duration and renewal
9. Termination clause

## Youth Mechanic Training

### Advanced Program for Ages 14-18

**Prerequisites:**
- Completed Earn-A-Bike
- Demonstrated interest and aptitude
- Good attendance and behavior
- Parent/guardian approval

**Curriculum:**

| Level | Skills | Hours |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **Apprentice** | All EAB skills mastered, shop procedures | 20 |
| **Mechanic I** | Wheel truing, hub service, headsets | 40 |
| **Mechanic II** | Bottom brackets, cable routing, full overhauls | 60 |
| **Mechanic III** | Diagnosis, customer service, teaching | 80 |

**Benefits:**
- Hourly stipend (if funding allows)
- Professional reference
- Portfolio of work
- Potential job placement
- Leadership opportunities

### Stipend/Employment Considerations

**Stipend model:**
- Paid per hour of training/work
- $10-15/hour typical
- Requires work permits (varies by state)
- Tax implications (1099 if >$600/year)

**Employment model:**
- Formal part-time employment
- Minimum wage laws apply
- Hour restrictions for minors
- Workers' compensation required

**Volunteer model:**
- No pay, but other benefits
- Free membership
- First pick of bikes/parts
- Training and certification
- Resume builder

## Group Rides with Youth

### Pre-Ride Checklist

**Bikes:**
- [ ] ABC check on all bikes
- [ ] Helmets properly fitted
- [ ] Bikes appropriate size for riders

**Participants:**
- [ ] Signed waivers on file
- [ ] Emergency contacts available
- [ ] Medical info reviewed
- [ ] Water bottles filled
- [ ] Sunscreen applied

**Staff:**
- [ ] Adequate ratio (1:4 or 1:5)
- [ ] First aid kit
- [ ] Cell phone with emergency numbers
- [ ] Basic repair kit (tube, pump, multi-tool)
- [ ] Route plan shared with office

**Route:**
- [ ] Scouted in advance
- [ ] Appropriate for skill level
- [ ] Avoids high-traffic areas
- [ ] Has bail-out options
- [ ] Bathroom access identified

### Ride Protocols

**Formation:**
- Lead adult at front (never passed)
- Sweep adult at back (never ahead of anyone)
- Additional adults distributed throughout
- Buddy system for participants

**Communication:**
- Teach hand signals before ride
- Verbal calls: "Car back!", "Stopping!", "Slowing!"
- Whistles for emergency stops
- Regroup points identified in advance

**Rules:**
- Stay with the group
- No passing the leader
- Single file on roads
- Stop at all intersections
- Helmets on at all times

### Incident Response

1. **Stop the group** in safe location
2. **Assess** the situation
3. **Administer first aid** if trained
4. **Call for help** if needed (911, then parents)
5. **Document** the incident
6. **Notify** program director and parents
7. **Complete** incident report same day

## Working with Diverse Youth

### Inclusive Practices

**Physical abilities:**
- Adaptive bikes available if possible
- Modifications for different abilities
- Focus on what they CAN do
- Partner with adaptive cycling organizations

**Learning differences:**
- Visual demonstrations
- Written and verbal instructions
- Hands-on practice
- Patience and repetition
- Break tasks into smaller steps

**Language:**
- Bilingual staff/volunteers if possible
- Visual guides and diagrams
- Simple, clear language
- Translation resources

**Economic:**
- Free or sliding scale programs
- Provide all necessary equipment
- Snacks included
- No cost barriers to participation

**Trauma-informed approach:**
- Predictable routines
- Clear expectations
- Safe environment
- Relationship building
- Patience with behavior

### Gender Inclusion

- Welcoming to all gender identities
- Gender-neutral language
- Consider girls/women/non-binary specific sessions
- Address any exclusionary behavior immediately
- Diverse staff representation
