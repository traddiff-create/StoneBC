## OPENING SECTION

We have some exciting news to share.

Stone Bicycle Coalition has just received a grant from the American Empathy Project—an initiative of the American Humanist Association—to bring a new event to Rapid City this spring. It's called **Pedal for Empathy**, and it's exactly what it sounds like: a community bike ride, a street cleanup, and a gathering of neighbors who care about our city.

On **Saturday, May 2, 2026**, we're inviting every Rapid City resident—whether you're a serious cyclist, a casual rider, or someone who just wants to be part of something meaningful—to join us for a morning on your bike, helping out, and meeting your community.

This isn't a race. It's not about fitness level or how fancy your bike is. It's about doing something together.

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS SECTION

### Why Pedal for Empathy? Why Now?

Here's something we know from talking to folks in Rapid City: people want to connect with their community. They want to do something good. But a lot of us feel isolated, especially from people we might not normally meet.

Cycling has this incredible power. When you're on a bike, moving through your neighborhood at human speed, you see things differently. You see the streets that need love. You notice other people. You remember that we're all trying to get somewhere, that we all share the same roads, the same air, the same city.

**Pedal for Empathy brings that together:**

The ride is a chance to move through Rapid City together, to see our community through shared experience. The cleanup is a chance to take action—to actually improve the streets we ride on. And the gathering afterward is just time to be neighbors, to share a meal, and to celebrate what we can do when we show up for each other.

The American Empathy Project funded this event because they believe in this work: bringing people together across difference, building connection, and creating local action. We're grateful for that support, and we're even more grateful that it gives us the chance to do this together.

## THE BIGGER PICTURE SECTION

### What Stone Bicycle Coalition Is All About

We know some folks might be thinking, "Stone Bicycle Coalition? Who are they?"

We're a grassroots organization in Rapid City focused on bike advocacy. We believe:

- Biking should be accessible to everyone, regardless of income or experience level
- Our streets should be safe and welcoming for cyclists and pedestrians
- Cycling is a way to connect with your community and take action
- Small organizations can do big things when people show up

Pedal for Empathy is exactly the kind of work we care about: bringing people together around something we all believe in (our community, our streets), and making it easy for people to participate.
