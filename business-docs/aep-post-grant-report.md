## 1. Project overview

Stone Bicycle Coalition is a Rapid City bike co-op based out of Minneluzahan Senior Citizen Center. We refurbish donated bikes for people who need transportation, run community rides, and look after Rapid City's bike paths. Pedal for Empathy was our first externally-funded event and SBC's first external grant.

Saturday May 2, we ran a free morning that did three things at once: coffee and donuts at Hanson-Larsen Memorial Park, a casual community ride on the Rapid City path system, and a hands-on trail cleanup along the way. We closed by donating $350 of the grant to Feeding South Dakota.

## 3. Empathy pillars in action

The application named three pillars. Each one showed up in something concrete on event day.

**1. Equitable access — safe streets for all cyclists.**
Nine riders, mixed ages, mixed skill levels, all on Rapid City's existing bike-path corridors at a social pace. Free helmets and lights were on offer, registration was zero-friction, and the ride did not require any prior experience. Kids came. The standout moment of the morning came from a parent telling us it was good for the kids to **see how necessary it is to help clean your living area** — that is the access pillar in its real form. The path is not just there; it is ours, and we keep it.

**2. Environmental stewardship — cleaning up cycling corridors.**
Nine volunteers, two completely-full 42-gallon contractor bags, about five miles of path. Roughly one bag every two-and-a-half miles. We did not have that baseline before; now we do, and we know what to aim for next time.

**3. Community connection — bringing Rapid City together around bikes.**
The morning ran on intergenerational presence. HLMP coffee at 10:30 AM. Acme Bikes hosting the wrap-up. Minneluzahan Senior Center as home base. Then we drove the empathy past the bike path and into the food pantry — a $350 donation to Feeding South Dakota for the same neighbors we ride past every week.

## 6. Personal reflection (Rory Stone, organizer)

This was SBC's first external grant. I was not sure ride + cleanup + donation would feel like one event. It does, once you let it run at community pace.

The moment that mattered most was not the ride. It was a parent at the cleanup, more or less in passing, saying it was good for the kids to see how necessary it is to help clean your living area. That sentence is the whole point. We did not have to teach empathy. The bags filled, the kids saw the bags fill, the lesson was already done.

What worked: zero-friction registration, a corridor people already know (HLMP → bike path → Acme), and letting Acme carry the social weight on the back end so I could focus on logistics on the front end.

What I would change: a second cleanup point so the bags do not all live on one rider's bike, an in-person sign-up sheet at HLMP to feed the volunteer list (Mobilize alone misses the walk-ups), and an earlier on-route water stop so energy gels are not the only fuel option on the long segments.

## 7. Sustained impact

The grant funded one morning. Several pieces outlast it:

- **Operational template.** Mobilize registration, QR media releases, JotForm receipt discipline, three-pillar framing — re-runnable for any future cleanup ride without reinventing anything.
- **Acme Bikes partnership.** A working relationship with the local bike shop pays off every season — referrals to The Quarry (SBC's wait-list bike program), shared volunteers, mutual promotion.
- **Feeding South Dakota relationship.** Hand-signed acknowledgement and an in-person $350 check delivery turned a line item into a real partnership.
- **Path stewardship cadence.** Baseline of ~1 bag per ~2.5 miles gives us a measurable target for quarterly cleanup rides going forward.
- **Visibility for SBC's nonprofit trajectory.** First external grant under our belt is a real step on the LLC → 501(c)(3) → member-owned cooperative path we are walking on a multi-year horizon.

Thank you to the AEP team for picking us out of 600+ applications and trusting a first-time grantee in Rapid City. We will keep showing up.
