### Project narrative (if asked for ~1–3 paragraphs)

> Stone Bicycle Coalition is a Rapid City bike co-op based out of Minneluzahan Senior Citizen Center. We refurbish donated bikes for people who need transportation, run community rides, and look after Rapid City's bike paths. Pedal for Empathy was our first externally-funded event and SBC's first external grant.
>
> Saturday May 2, we ran a free morning that did three things at once: coffee and donuts at Hanson-Larsen Memorial Park, a casual community ride on the Rapid City path system, and a hands-on trail cleanup along the way. Nine riders/volunteers, ~5 miles of path, and two completely-full 42-gallon contractor bags of trash. We closed by donating $350 of the grant to Feeding South Dakota.
>
> The application named three empathy pillars (equitable access, environmental stewardship, community connection). Each one showed up in something concrete on event day. The standout moment came from a parent telling us it was good for the kids to see "how necessary it is to help clean your living area" — that sentence is the whole point. We did not have to teach empathy. The bags filled, the kids saw the bags fill, the lesson was already done.

### Personal reflection (if asked separately)

> This was SBC's first external grant. I was not sure ride + cleanup + donation would feel like one event. It does, once you let it run at community pace.
>
> The moment that mattered most was not the ride — it was a parent at the cleanup, more or less in passing, saying it was good for the kids to see how necessary it is to help clean your living area. That sentence is the whole point.
>
> What worked: zero-friction registration, a corridor people already know (HLMP → bike path → Acme), and letting Acme carry the social weight on the back end so I could focus on logistics on the front end.
>
> What I would change: a second cleanup point so the bags do not all live on one rider's bike, an in-person sign-up sheet at HLMP to feed the volunteer list (Mobilize alone misses the walk-ups), and an earlier on-route water stop so energy gels are not the only fuel option on the long segments.
