## What Is Kidical Mass?

A national movement of free, family-friendly group bike rides. Started in Eugene, Oregon in April 2008, now active in 12+ North American cities plus Europe. The name is a play on "Critical Mass" — same group-riding concept but designed for kids and families.

**Core philosophy:** "The bike movement has grown up, and now it has kids!" — Shane MacRhodes, founder

---

## How It Works

### Ride Format
- Free, no-drop rides (nobody gets left behind)
- 2-5 miles, easy pace
- Start at a park, end at a kid-friendly destination (ice cream shop, splash pad, pool)
- Routes use residential streets, bike boulevards, and off-street paths
- All bike types welcome: trailers, cargo bikes, trikes, trail-a-bikes, tandems, folders
- Rides end with treats and social time

### Volunteer Structure (minimal)
- **Ride leader** — front of group, sets pace, coordinates stops
- **Sweeper** — stays at back, ensures nobody falls behind
- **Escort/ambassadors** (optional) — help at intersections, assist families leaving early
- Adults without kids welcome as volunteers

### Logistics
- **No permits required** — group follows all traffic laws, doesn't intentionally block traffic
- **No formal registration** — show up and ride
- **Liability:** Varies by chapter. Some use waivers, others keep it informal. Standard disclaimer: "each family is responsible for their child's safety"
- **Helmets:** Strongly encouraged, legally required for children in many states (check SD law)
- **Cadence:** Monthly (national model is 3rd Saturday of each month)

### Outreach Strategy
- Deliberately reaches **beyond the cycling community**
- Flyers at elementary schools, libraries, PTAs, grocery stores
- Email lists, social media, local media
- Post-ride photo sharing builds community + promotes next ride
- Kids involved in promotion and planning

## National Organization

- **Website:** kidicalmass.org (dormant since ~2022, basic but functional)
- **Contact:** info@kidicalmass.org
- **Facebook:** Kidical Mass Organizers Group (peer support for chapter leads)
- **Google Group:** Chapter discussion forum
- **No formal affiliation** — start riding, then email to get listed in directory

### How to Start a Chapter (from their FAQ)
1. Select a meeting date/time (weekend afternoons recommended)
2. Choose a starting location (park)
3. Identify an ending destination (ice cream, park, community event)
4. Plan a safe, family-friendly route between start and end
5. Publicize via email, social media, flyers, schools
6. Ride and share photos
7. Contact national to get listed in directory

## Gaps in Their Model
- No formal toolkit, templates, or starter kit
- No insurance/legal guidance beyond "follow traffic laws"
- No branding guidelines or logo assets
- National site hasn't been actively maintained
- No structured onboarding for new chapters

## Fit with StoneBC — Rapid City

### Why This Works for Us
- **AEP grant alignment** — family cycling access is core to our mission
- **Pedal for Empathy tie-in** — May 2 event could be first family ride or promotional launchpad
- **Bike donation program** — pairs naturally with kids who need bikes
- **App integration** — StoneBC app can host family routes + event listings
- **Low overhead** — 2 volunteers, a park, and a destination. No permits, no budget needed
- **Rapid City has great routes** — bike path system, parks, kid-friendly destinations
