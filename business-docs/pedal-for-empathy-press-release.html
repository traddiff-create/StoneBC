Stone Bicycle Coalition Awarded Grant to Host "The Empathy Ride" as Part of National Day of Action

Rapid City, SD — Stone Bicycle Coalition is proud to announce it has been selected as a grantee of the American Humanist Association's American Empathy Project, receiving $1,000 to host The Empathy Ride on Saturday, May 2, 2026 — joining hundreds of community organizations across the country in a national day of action rooted in compassion and collective care.

The American Empathy Project is a nationwide initiative distributing $100,000 in grants to changemakers committed to putting empathy into action. Stone Bicycle Coalition was one of the organizations selected from over 600 applications to bring that mission to life in the Black Hills.

The Empathy Ride will take place on Saturday, May 2, 2026, beginning at the HLMP Bandshell Volleyball Courts in Rapid City at 10:30 AM. Riders will follow a casual-pace route along the Rapid City Bike Path — a community ride and street cleanup celebrating connection over division. The ride is open to all skill levels and families, and is free to participate. A post-ride community gathering with snacks will follow, along with a $350 donation to Feeding South Dakota.

"Bicycles have always been instruments of freedom, connection, and equity. In a moment when so many of us are feeling the weight of division and cruelty, we want to offer something simple and human — the chance to ride together, side by side, through the community we love. That's empathy in motion."
— Nicole Stone, Co-Founder, Stone Bicycle Coalition

Stone Bicycle Coalition was founded by Nicole and Rory Stone to advance active transportation, revive and redistribute bikes to community members in need, and build a more connected Black Hills region. The coalition's work is grounded in the belief that cycling is not just recreation — it is access, health, sustainability, and community.

The Empathy Ride is part of a coordinated national day of action ahead of the National Day of Reason on May 4. Events like this one are happening in communities across the country, all on the same day, sending a unified message: compassion is stronger than cruelty, and people are still good.

About Stone Bicycle Coalition

Stone Bicycle Coalition is a grassroots community organization in Rapid City, South Dakota, dedicated to advancing active transportation, reviving and redistributing bicycles to community members in need, and building a more connected Black Hills region. Founded by Rory and Nicole Stone, the coalition operates community rides, route development, bike repair workshops, and advocacy initiatives. Learn more at stonebicyclecoalition.com.

About the American Empathy Project

The American Empathy Project is an initiative of the American Humanist Association distributing $100,000 in grants to community organizations putting empathy into action across the United States. Selected from over 600 applications, grantees host events on a coordinated national day of action ahead of the National Day of Reason.
