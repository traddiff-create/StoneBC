## Purpose

Run check-in in a way that keeps the City liability waiver and AEP media release separate.

- **City liability waiver:** required for every participant.
- **AEP media release:** required only for people whose identifiable face may appear in photos/videos shared with AEP/AHA.
- **Photo rule:** identifiable faces are only captured for participants marked `OK for photos`.

## Check-In Table Setup

At the HLMP check-in table, set out:

- City liability waivers in one stack.
- AHA paper media releases in a separate stack.
- QR sign visible from the line.
- Check-in sheet on a clipboard.
- Pens, painter's tape, and two folders:
  - `Signed City Liability Waivers`
  - `Signed AEP Media Releases`

Place the other QR signs at:

- Coffee/donuts area.
- Pre-ride briefing spot.
- Acme wrap-up table.

Place the **AHA membership QR signs** at:

- Coffee/donuts table (high-dwell area).
- Acme wrap-up table.

---

## Check-In Script

Say this to each participant:

> First, everyone signs the City liability waiver. Second, if your face may appear in photos or video we share with the American Empathy Project, scan this QR code for the AEP media release. If you do not want face photos, tell us and we will mark that here.

For minors:

> A parent or legal guardian needs to sign both forms for minors.

For anyone without a phone:

> We have the AEP media release on paper too.

---

## Check-In Sheet Values

Use these values consistently:

- **City waiver:** `Y` after paper form is signed.
- **AEP media:** `QR`, `Paper`, `Declined`, or `Not needed`.
- **Photo preference:** `OK for photos`, `No face photos`, or `Paper media form`.

If someone declines the AEP release, mark photo preference as `No face photos`.

---

## Photo Lead Rules

The photo lead may take:

- Faces of participants marked `OK for photos`.
- Group photos only after the waiver lead confirms the group is cleared.
- Non-identifying photos anytime: hands at work, bikes, supplies, cleanup bags, signs, route details, backs/helmets, and wide shots without identifiable faces.

The photo lead should avoid:

- Identifiable faces of anyone marked `No face photos`.
- Bystanders.
- Vulnerable or service-recipient situations unless explicit permission is given.
